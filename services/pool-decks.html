Premium Concrete Pool Deck Installation

Safe, Stylish, and Inviting Poolside Surfaces

Your pool deck is the stage for everything that happens around your pool â from lazy summer afternoons to lively gatherings with family and friends. A well-designed and professionally installed concrete pool deck elevates the entire experience, creating a safe, beautiful surface that holds up to constant wet-dry cycles, chlorine exposure, and Ohio's demanding freeze-thaw seasons. Robson Concrete & Construction installs premium concrete pool decks throughout Northeast Ohio, engineered specifically for the poolside environment. We focus on proper drainage away from the pool and structure, non-slip texture options that are safe when wet, and finishes that stay comfortable underfoot even in peak summer sun. Whether you're installing a new pool deck or resurfacing an existing one, we deliver results that look resort-worthy and perform for decades.

Our Pool Deck Services

New Pool Deck Installation

Complete pool deck installation from design through final finishing. We coordinate with pool contractors and handle all concrete work, expansion joints, and drainage to manufacturer and code requirements.

Pool Deck Resurfacing

Worn, stained, or cracked pool decks can often be restored through resurfacing. We apply durable overlay systems that refresh the appearance and improve safety without full replacement cost.

Non-Slip Finishes

Safety is paramount around pools. We finish every pool deck with textures specifically designed to provide grip when wet, dramatically reducing slip-and-fall risk for swimmers of all ages.

Cool Deck Options

Standard concrete can get uncomfortably hot in direct sun. We offer light-colored finishes and specialty cool deck coatings that stay significantly cooler underfoot on hot summer days.

Integrated Drainage

Pool decks require precise slope and drainage design to direct water away from the pool shell, home foundation, and landscaping. We engineer drainage from the ground up for long-term performance.
